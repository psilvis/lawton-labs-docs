Privacy Policy

1. Overview

Gradual ("the App") is operated by Lawton Labs ("we," "us," or "our"). The App is built with a privacy-first approach. Your health data is stored securely on your device and synced via Apple's iCloud service using CloudKit. Your data is never stored on Lawton Labs servers. Certain optional features involve third-party processing as described below.

This policy is designed to comply with the Washington My Health My Data Act (RCW 19.373). We collect consumer health data only with your separate, affirmative consent, which is obtained independently from any general terms acceptance.

This policy is designed to comply with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA). We collect personal information only with your knowledge and consent, and for purposes that a reasonable person would consider appropriate.

This policy is designed to comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. Lawton Labs is the data controller for your personal data. We process your data lawfully, fairly, and transparently.

This policy is designed to comply with Japan's Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI / 個人情報の保護に関する法律). We handle personal information appropriately and use it only for specified purposes with your consent.

This policy is designed to comply with Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (PDPA). We collect, use, and disclose personal data only with your consent and for purposes that a reasonable person would consider appropriate. A Data Protection Officer has been designated as required by the PDPA.

2. Categories of Health Data Collected

The App collects and stores the following categories of consumer health personal personal personal personal data on your device:

  • Physical activity data — Steps, distance, active energy, workout sessions, and exercise minutes.
  • Cardiovascular metrics — Heart rate, resting heart rate, heart rate variability (HRV), VO2max.
  • Sleep data — Sleep duration, sleep stages, and sleep quality scores.
  • Body measurements — Height, weight, body fat percentage (when available via HealthKit).
  • Lab biomarkers — Values extracted from lab reports you import (e.g., cholesterol, glucose, hormones, vitamins).
  • Mood and well-being — Daily mood ratings and associated tags you record.
  • Supplement and habit tracking — Supplements, dosages, habits, and adherence logs you create.
  • Derived and inferred data — Gradual Age (composite biological age score), readiness scores, sleep quality scores, and AI-generated health insights. These are inferences drawn from your health data and are treated as consumer health datapersonal informationpersonal datapersonal data under this policy.

The App also collects non-health data:

  • Profile information — Your name, age, and biological sex entered during onboarding.
  • Apple Sign-In credentials — Apple user ID and email for authentication.
  • Notification preferences — Your chosen reminder settings.

All health data listed above is encrypted on-device and synced securely via Apple iCloud (CloudKit). Your data is stored in your personal iCloud account and is never stored on Lawton Labs servers.

3. Categories of Sources

Health data is collected from the following sources:

  • Apple HealthKit — Physical activity, cardiovascular, sleep, and body measurement data (with your explicit permission).
  • Manual entry — Mood check-ins, supplement logs, habit tracking, and manually entered biomarkers.
  • Imported lab reports — Biomarker values extracted from PDF lab reports you upload.
  • Strava — Workout and activity data (when you connect your Strava account).
  • AI inference — Derived scores and insights generated by analyzing your health data.

4. Purpose of Collection

We collect consumer health datapersonal informationpersonal datapersonal data for the following purposes:

  • Personalized health tracking — Displaying your health metrics, trends, and progress over time.
  • Biological age computation — Calculating your Gradual Age composite score across cardiovascular, strength, and recovery dimensions.
  • AI-powered analysis — Generating personalized health insights and recommendations (optional, requires separate consent at point of use).
  • Lab report parsing — Extracting biomarker values from uploaded lab reports (optional, requires separate consent at point of use).
  • Habit and supplement tracking — Monitoring adherence and streaks for your protocols.

We do not sell, rent, or share your health data for advertising, marketing, or any purpose unrelated to providing the App's features.

5. Third-Party Processors

The following third-party services may process your data:

Apple iCloud (CloudKit)

All health data is encrypted and synced to your personal iCloud account. Apple processes this data under your iCloud terms. Lawton Labs does not have server-side access to your iCloud data.

OpenAI

When you enable AI features, the following data may be sent to OpenAI's API:

  • AI Insights: Health metrics relevant to your current screen (e.g., sleep, heart rate, activity), your age range, and biological sex. No names, emails, or device identifiers are shared.
  • Lab Import: Your lab report PDF, which may contain biomarker values and personal identifiers.

Under OpenAI's API data usage policy, data sent via the API is not used to train OpenAI's models. Data is transmitted via encrypted HTTPS. A separate consent dialog is shown before any data is sent to OpenAI.

Cloudflare

API requests transit through Cloudflare's network. Request data is not stored by Cloudflare beyond transient processing.

Amplitude

Anonymous usage analytics only. No health data, biomarker values, or personally identifiable information is sent to Amplitude.

RevenueCat

Apple user ID and subscription status for managing in-app purchases. No health data is shared.

Firebase Crashlytics

Crash reports including device type, OS version, and stack traces. No health data is included in crash reports.

6. Apple HealthKit Data

Health data accessed through Apple HealthKit is handled according to Apple's HealthKit guidelines:

  • Data is only accessed after you grant explicit permission.
  • HealthKit data is read only — the App does not write data to HealthKit.
  • HealthKit data is synced via your personal iCloud account using Apple's CloudKit. This data is stored in your private iCloud container and is never stored on Lawton Labs servers or transmitted to third parties (except as described in the AI features section above, with your separate consent).
  • HealthKit data is never used for advertising or marketing purposes.

7. Strava Integration

When you connect your Strava account:

  • Authentication uses OAuth 2.0 — the App never sees or stores your Strava password.
  • Only activity data (type, duration, distance, heart rate) is retrieved from Strava's API.
  • Retrieved activity data is cached locally on your device for up to 7 days per Strava's API Agreement.
  • You can disconnect Strava at any time from the Profile screen. Disconnecting deletes all cached Strava data and derived metrics from your device.

8. Data Storage and Security

  • Sensitive data (API keys, tokens) is stored using expo-secure-store, which uses the iOS Keychain for encrypted storage.
  • Health metrics stored in SQLite are encrypted with application-layer AES-256-GCM. The encryption key is stored in the iOS Keychain.
  • Your data syncs to your personal iCloud account via Apple's CloudKit. This data is stored in a private CloudKit container accessible only to you. No health data is stored on servers operated by Lawton Labs.
  • Data export is available in JSON format from Settings > Data & Privacy.

9. Data Retention and Deletion

  • All data is stored on your device and synced to your personal iCloud account. Data persists until you delete it.
  • You can delete all data at any time using the "Delete All Data" option in Settings. This removes data from your device and iCloud.
  • Uninstalling the App removes all locally stored data. Data in your iCloud account may persist until deleted via the App or iCloud settings.
  • Data sent to OpenAI for AI features is processed transiently and is not retained by OpenAI for model training purposes, per OpenAI's API data usage policies.
  • Because Lawton Labs does not have server-side access to your health data, deletion is performed entirely through the App or your iCloud account settings.

Specific retention periods:

  • Health data — Retained on your device and iCloud for as long as you use the App.
  • Anonymous analytics — Retained by Amplitude for up to 2 years.
  • Crash reports — Retained by Firebase Crashlytics for up to 90 days.
  • AI-processed data — Not retained by OpenAI after processing is complete.

9a. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the UK GDPR, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a) and Article 9(2)(a)) — Health data processing, HealthKit access, AI lab parsing, and AI Insights are based on your explicit consent, which you can withdraw at any time via Settings > Data & Privacy > Manage Consent.
  • Performance of a contract (Article 6(1)(b)) — Account management, subscription processing, and core app functionality are necessary to provide the service you signed up for.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) — Anonymous analytics and crash reporting help us improve app stability and user experience. We have assessed that these interests do not override your rights given the data is fully anonymized.

10. Consent and Withdrawal

We obtain your consent for health data collection in the following ways:

  • Health data collection consent — Obtained during onboarding via a separate, affirmative checkbox on the data sync screen, independent from any general terms acceptance.
  • AI processing consent — Obtained at point of use via a consent dialog each time data would be sent to OpenAI (for AI Insights or lab report parsing). This consent names the third party, describes what data is shared, and explains how to withdraw.

How to withdraw consent:

  • AI Processing: Disable AI Insights in Settings > Data & Privacy > Manage Consent, or toggle it off in the Manage Consent screen within the App.
  • Health data collection: Use "Delete All Data" in Settings to remove all health data from your device and iCloud. Because health data collection is required to provide the App's core functionality, withdrawing this consent requires deleting your data.
  • By email: Send a withdrawal request to privacy@gradual.health.
